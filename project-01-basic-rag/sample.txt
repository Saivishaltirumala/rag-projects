IPL 2026 Season Overview
The 2026 Indian Premier League (TATA IPL 2026) is the 19th season of the IPL. The league stage featured 70 matches across multiple venues in India. The season has been described as the most explosive ever, with breakneck scoring rates and frequent 200-plus totals. The league stage ran from late March to May 24, 2026. The playoffs begin on May 26, and the final is scheduled for May 31 at the Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad.

Orange Cap Race - Top Run Scorers in IPL 2026
1. B Sai Sudharsan (Gujarat Titans) - 638 runs
2. Shubman Gill (Gujarat Titans) - 616 runs
3. Heinrich Klaasen (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 606 runs
4. Vaibhav Sooryavanshi (Rajasthan Royals) - 579 runs
5. Mitchell Marsh (Lucknow Super Giants) - 563 runs

Sai Sudharsan has been the standout batter of IPL 2026, leading the Orange Cap race with 638 runs for the Gujarat Titans. His teammate Shubman Gill is close behind with 616 runs, making GT's top order the most prolific in the tournament. Heinrich Klaasen of SRH has been in destructive form with 606 runs and an impressive strike rate. Young Vaibhav Sooryavanshi of Rajasthan Royals has been a revelation with 579 runs, announcing himself on the big stage. Mitchell Marsh rounds out the top 5 with 563 runs for LSG despite their poor team performance.

Anshul Kamboj has been CSK's brightest spot with 21 wickets. Rashid Khan continues to be a match-winner for GT with 19 wickets at an excellent economy of 8.10, while young Eshan Malinga has impressed for SRH with 19 wickets.

Key League Stage Results
Gujarat Titans defeated Chennai Super Kings by 89 runs in a dominant performance on May 21, securing a top-2 finish. This was one of the biggest victories of the season. Sunrisers Hyderabad beat Royal Challengers Bengaluru by 55 runs on May 22 in the last league match.

IPL 2026 Playoffs Schedule
Qualifier 1: Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) vs Gujarat Titans (GT) - May 26, 2026, 7:30 PM IST at HPCA Stadium, Dharamsala
Eliminator: Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH) vs Rajasthan Royals (RR) - May 27, 2026, 7:30 PM IST at Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium, New Chandigarh
Qualifier 2: Loser of Qualifier 1 vs Winner of Eliminator - May 29, 2026, 7:30 PM IST at Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium, New Chandigarh
Final: Winner of Qualifier 1 vs Winner of Qualifier 2 - May 31, 2026, 7:30 PM IST at Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad

The winner of Qualifier 1 between RCB and GT will directly advance to the final. The loser gets a second chance in Qualifier 2 against the Eliminator winner. The Eliminator between SRH and RR is a do-or-die match where the loser is knocked out. All playoff matches start at 7:30 PM IST.

Team Highlights
Gujarat Titans have been the most balanced side with both the Orange Cap holder (Sai Sudharsan) and a Purple Cap contender (Kagiso Rabada) in their ranks. Shubman Gill's captaincy and batting have been instrumental. Rashid Khan continues to deliver in the middle overs.

Royal Challengers Bengaluru topped the table with the best net run rate. Bhuvneshwar Kumar's Purple Cap-winning bowling has been their backbone. Their bowling attack has been the most disciplined in the tournament with the best overall economy rate.

Sunrisers Hyderabad's batting has been powered by Heinrich Klaasen's explosive innings. They finished third despite having the same points as RCB and GT. Young pacer Eshan Malinga has been a find for them.

Rajasthan Royals scraped into the playoffs at fourth position, largely thanks to Vaibhav Sooryavanshi's breakout season with the bat. They will need to win three consecutive matches to lift the trophy.
